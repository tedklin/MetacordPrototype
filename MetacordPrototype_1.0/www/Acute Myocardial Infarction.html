When your blood vessels become blocked, plaque builds. The blood is supposed to transport oxygen to the organs in your body through these vessels. Without the needed oxygen, the tissue will become damaged or in extreme cases, die. Acute myocardial infarction can be life-threatening. Chest pain is the most common symptom of a heart attack but you may also experience: nausea, sweating, shortness of breath, vomiting, anxiety, dizziness, fast heart rate, coughing, and periodical pain in certain areas of the upper body such as the chest, jaw, and back. Patients with high blood pressure, high cholesterol and diabetes are at higher risk for this disease.
