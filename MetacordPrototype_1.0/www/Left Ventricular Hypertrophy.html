This occurs when the muscle wall on the left side of your heart is enlarged or thickened. When a patient has high blood pressure or a heart condition it causes the left side of you heart to work harder. The extra work causes the wall muscles to thicken larger than they should. When the wall muscle becomes too thick it will lose its elasticity and fail to pump properly. Although you may be healthy this disease will put you at a higher risk of having a heart attack or stroke. If you have this disease you may be experiencing: fatigue, shortness of breath, chest pain after being active, quickened heartbeats or palpitations, and dizziness.
